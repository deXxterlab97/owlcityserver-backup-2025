1:58 PM
Slowly and carefully Brielle picked her way through the woods and up the mountain. Right now, she just wanted to be alone. She was headed toward the one place that still gave her a sense of safety, of comfort, of home. She and Jon spent lot of time in that cave in their early teenage years, and she loved how secluded it was. They made sure to keep it a secret, and she came here sometimes to feel alone. As she crawled through the entrance and lit a candle, she gasped. All the old crates and boxes they used as tables, chairs, and bookcases lay strewn about the floor. This wasn't the first time this happened, as sometimes a wild animal would spend the night in the comfort of the cave, so Brielle got to work cleaning up. She put the table right side up and pushed the chairs in, and then tried to set up the big crate they used as the bookcase. But then she noticed something; there was an opening in the wall behind the case she hadn't known about. Sure, there were off-shoots of the main room she and Jon had explored in the past, but this was brand new to her. So, she set off down it trying hoping the adventure would distract her.
1:59 PM
Slowly she made her way down the long snaking tunnel. At the points the tunnel made a rather steep descent she went and broke down the crates they used as chairs to make some stairs so she wouldn't fall. Finally, she reached a drop too steep to descend, even with some homemade steps. She considered giving up, but exploring the cave put her racing thoughts to rest, so she returned to the surface to grab a rope. As she approached the surface, her mind began to race again. She wished things were different between her and Jon, that she didn't have to hurt him like she did. She thought of her sister, and the rift that had grown between them. She hoped the pain would be worth it, and that they could finally begin to heal. The emotions overwhelmed her, and she began to suspect that when she went down that tunnel, she wouldn't surface again. As she grabbed the rope, she left a note on the table in its place. Descending the tunnel again, she tied the rope to a rock and then around her waist. Slowly, very slowly, she began to climb down the rock ledge. She made it about half way down. She lost her grip and the rope was too old to be of any use. She fell 20 feet into the freezing water, and lost consciousness quickly. And that's when she really began to explore. (edited)

When Brielle didn't come home, her grandmother didn't think much of it. Brielle could be absent minded at times, and had a tendency to spend the night with friends without notice. When she didn't return the next evening though, her grandmother grew concerned, and contacted the police. Search teams were organized, and they combed through the surrounding forests. Wanted posters were put up, and authorities in the surrounding towns were notified. Dogs were called out, and after after a few days boats started checking the bottom of the harbor. They thought they found a trail in the woods, but lost it at the base of the mountains. When Grandmother called to let her know Brielle was missing, Jane hopped on the next bus home. She and Grandmother were distraught, and did their best to reassure each other Brielle would be home safe soon. A mutual friend telegrammed the ship Jon was on, and let him know what happened. Jon was absolutely distraught. He loved Brielle so much, and was crushed to have to leave so soon after the breakup on a contract. The news reached him as the ship was on the return trip, and he spent all his time wishing he could be home searching for her. At night, it was as if his mind left his body and flew back home to search for her. He saw and blue and red overhead as the police lights got caught on the clouds. He dreamt of searching the forest and shore, of checking under porches and in long forgotten houses. (edited)
